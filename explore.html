In Winschoten

Rosarium Winschoten

Location: Mr. D.U. Stikkerlaan 1a, Winschoten

About the place: One of the largest rose gardens in the Netherlands, with 20,000 roses (320 varieties) inside a large park with lakes and walking paths.

Opening Hours:

  • Sat–Sun: 10:00–21:00
  • Mon–Tue: Closed
  • Wed–Fri: 11:00–21:00

What you can do:

  • Walk through themed rose gardens.
  • Relax near ponds and fountains.
  • Visit the small pavilion café.
  • Photography & nature walks.

Entry fee: Free

Tips:

  • Best time: June–August (full bloom).
  • Visit in the evening for quieter atmosphere.
  • Combine with nearby Stadspark walk.

Museum Stoomgemaal Winschoten

Location: Oostereinde 4, Winschoten

About the place: A historic steam pumping station (1890s–1913 era) turned into a museum showing how water was managed in the Netherlands.

Opening Hours:

  • 1st Saturday of month: 09:00–15:00
  • Wed: 09:00–12
  • Otherwise mostly closed.

What you can do:

  • See working steam engines.
  • Learn Dutch water engineering history.
  • Join guided tours & demos.

Entry fee: €7.50 adults,
Free for children under 12

Tips:

  • Check schedule before visiting (limited hours!).
  • Visit on demonstration days for best experience.
  • Good for history/engineering lovers.

Vennekerk

during services or events

Tips:

Marktpleinkerk

Location: City center, Marktplein, Winschoten

About the place: A central Protestant church located directly on the main square. It’s one of the most recognizable historic buildings in Winschoten.

Opening Hours: Usually open during services/events,
Limited public access otherwise

What you can do:

  • Admire architecture.
  • Attend church services or concerts.
  • Take photos from the square.

Entry fee: Free / donation

Tips:

  • Best visited during Marktplein events.
  • Exterior is always worth seeing even if closed.
