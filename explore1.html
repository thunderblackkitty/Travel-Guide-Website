Marktplein

Location: Central square, Winschoten

About the place: The main public square where markets, festivals, and events are held.

Opening Hours: Always open

What you can do:

  • Visit weekly markets.
  • Attend festivals.
  • Sit at cafés.

Entry fee: Free

Tips:

  • Check for local events
  • Best visited during market days

Winschoten Train & Bus Station

Location: Stationsweg, Winschoten

About the place: The main transport hub connecting Winschoten to cities like Groningen.

Opening Hours: Open daily (transport schedules apply)

What you can do:

  • Travel by train.
  • Access buses to nearby towns.
  • Quick stop for amenities.

Entry fee: Free

Tips:

  • Direct train to Groningen (~30 min).
  • Good starting point for exploring the area.

Windmill Edens

Location: Nassaustraat, Winschoten

About the place: A historic Dutch windmill (1763), one of the oldest and most famous in the region.

Opening Hours: Limited

What you can do:

  • See traditional milling machinery.
  • Learn about Dutch windmill history.
  • Photography.

Entry fee: Small fee or donation

Tips:

  • Visit when open inside for full experience.
  • Great photo spot.

Windmill Berg

Location: Winschoten

About the place: A traditional windmill still reflecting the industrial
past of the town.

Opening Hours: Limited / occasional openings

What you can do:

  • View exterior.
  • Learn local milling history.
  • Learn about traditional windmill mechanics

Entry fee: Usually free

Tips:

  • Combine with other windmills in one trip
  • Good spot for taking picture.

Windmill Dijkstra

Location: Winschoten

About the place: Another historic mill showing the importance of grain milling in the area.

Opening Hours: Limited access

What you can do:

  • Sightseeing.
  • Photography.

Entry fee: Free

Tips:

  • Best visited in daylight
  • Watch the sails turn when the mill is operating.

De Klinker Library

Location: Inside Cultuurhuis De Klinker, Winschoten

About the place: A modern public library with books, study areas, and community programs.

Opening Hours: Typically 10:00–17:00

What you can do:

  • Use internet, read & study.
  • Attend workshops.

Entry fee: Free (membership needed for borrowing)

Tips:

  • Quiet place to relax.
  • Good stop if weather is bad.

Nearby & Around Winschoten

Blauwestad / Oldambtmeer

Location: 10 min from Winschoten

About the place: A modern water recreation area with a large
artificial lake.

Opening Hours: Always Open

What you can do:

  • Swimming.
  • Sailing & baoting.
  • Walking & cycling.

Entry fee: Free

Tips:

  • Great in summer.
  • Bring food for picnic.

Border Bridge (Pieter Smit Bridge)

Location: Near Dutch-German border

About the place: A bridge connecting the Netherlands and Germany, symbolic and scenic.

Opening Hours: Always open

What you can do:

  • Walk across countries.
  • Take photos.
  • Stand in two countries at once.

Entry fee: Free

Tips:

  • Good for quick stop.
  • Nice sunset views.

Reiderhaven Marina

Location: Beerta village

About the place: A peaceful marina near the lake, popular with boat owners and visitors.

Opening Hours: Always open

What you can do:

  • Relax by the water.
  • Watch boats.
  • Walk around.

Entry fee: Free

Tips:

  • Very calm (शांत) and less crowded.
  • Good picnic spot.

Dinopark Estate Tenaxx

Location: Wedde

About the place: A fun prehistoric park with life-size dinosaur models and nature trails.

Opening Hours: Seasonal (mostly spring–autumn)

What you can do:

  • Walk among dinosaurs.
  • Explore forest trails.
  • Family activities.

Entry fee: €10–€15

Tips:

  • Great for kids.
  • Wear comfortable shoes.
